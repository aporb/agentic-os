# Weekly Review

Close out the week with a structured look back. What shipped, what didn't, and what changes next week.

## When to Use

- End of your work week (typically Friday afternoon)
- You say "weekly review", "wrap up the week", "end of week review"

## Steps

1. **Scan the week** — Read all journal entries from the past 7 days. List everything you worked on.
2. **Categorize** — Sort into:
   - **Shipped**: Things that are done and out the door
   - **In progress**: Things you started but didn't finish
   - **Dropped**: Things you planned but never started
   - **Surprises**: Things that came up that you didn't plan for
3. **Honest assessment** — Answer three questions:
   - What am I proud of this week?
   - What drained my energy?
   - What would I do differently?
4. **Set next week's top 3** — Based on what's in progress and what matters most, name the three things that MUST ship next week.
5. **Save** — Write to `journal/YYYY-MM-DD-weekly-review.md`

## Output

**File**: `journal/YYYY-MM-DD-weekly-review.md`

## Pitfalls

- **All work, no reflection**: The value isn't the task list — it's the three questions. Don't skip the honest assessment.
- **Recurring dropped items**: If the same thing keeps getting dropped, either it's not actually important or something is structurally preventing it. Address the pattern.
- **Overloading next week**: Three priorities. Not seven. Not twelve. Three.
